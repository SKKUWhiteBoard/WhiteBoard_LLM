Offshoring, also known as offshore outsourcing, has been taking place for decades. The extension of offshoring from U.S. manufacturers to service providers has heightened public policy concerns. Offshoring is contributing to the slow rebound of the labor market from the 2007-2009 recession. Some economists agree that it benefits the nation as a whole by enabling firms to sell a greater variety of higher quality products to U.s. consumers at lower prices. But some question whether this scenario applies to services offsh outsourcing.
